à propos

Le paradigme des interfaces WYSIWYG (What You See Is What You Get) a profondément marqué les pratiques du design graphique et de la publication depuis quarante ans. En parallèle, le paradigme du document structuré, fondé sur la programmation, a façonné des domaines comme la publication scientifique ou le Web.

Ces deux approches, souvent pensées séparément, connaissent aujourd’hui des formes d’hybridation inédites, portées par des designers graphiques qui articulent code, interface graphique WYSIWYG et documents structurés. Ces journées d’étude souhaite explorer ces zones d’intersection: comment et où se déploient-elles? Que puisent-elles dans chaque paradigme? Que laissent-elles de côté ou qu’inventent-elles de nouveau?

Cette évolution nous invite également à réinterroger l’histoire de ces paradigmes, généralement étudiés séparément, afin de comprendre comment ceux-ci ont déjà pu dialoguer ou, au contraire, comment ils se sont construits en opposition.

When Code Meets the Interface: Hybridizing Graphic Design Tools

The WYSIWYG paradigm (What You See Is What You Get) is well known to graphic designers. It sped up the adoption of computers in the profession and defined it for the last 40 years. This paradigm also became the de facto standard in mainstream layout tools and text editors (Word, Canva). In parallel, another paradigm has existed since the beginning of computers, that of structured documents, which is based on programming. However, this inherently textual practice has traditionally been far removed from graphic designers' know-how. It is nevertheless still largely in use today, in academic publishing (LaTeX), but also as the technical basis of the Web (HTML & CSS).

In recent years, more and more graphic designers have started adopting programming (creative coding, webdesign). This new tooling has allowed them to design outside of traditional desktop publishing software. Even more recently, from this community, new tools are being created in which both code and WYSIWYG interaction now coexist. Drawing from both paradigms, these new graphic design practices are not just juxtaposing code and graphical user interface, structured document and WYSIWYG: instead they question their limits and relevance. This symposium will explore these new hybrid ways of doing graphic design. How and where do they flourish? What do they draw from each paradigm? What do they leave out or invent anew? These topical practices also invite us to reexamine the history of these paradigms, generally studied separately, to understand how they might have learned from each other or if, instead, they might have developed in opposition.

French talks will be live-translated into English. A limited number of translation headsets will be available. Please contact us if you would like one during the symposium (contact@julie-blanc.fr, nmaudet@unistra.fr)

Son travail s’attache à concevoir des outils ouverts favorisant l’autonomie des usagers et la mutualisation des savoir-faire. Il s’inscrit dans une réflexion plus large sur les pratiques collaboratives et les formes contemporaines de publication, entre expérimentation graphique, recherche collective et création numérique.
